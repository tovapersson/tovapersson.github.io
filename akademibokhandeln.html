Background

Since 1971, Akademibokhandeln has held a vital mission to make literature accessible. However, children's reading habits are changing with increased screen time. Research shows that the desire to read is formed early and is strongly influenced by parental engagement.

The challenge was to leverage SEO and content marketing to reach more parents and encourage them to purchase children's books regularly, rather than just on single occasions.

Approach

We developed an SEO-driven ecosystem centered around the children’s book club "Läsligan", focusing on meeting parents throughout the entire customer journey:

  • Discovery: Informative articles, guides, and book tips capturing searches related to children's reading, screen time, and literacy challenges.
  • Consideration: Inspiring content, reviews, and stories from children and parents demonstrating how reading fits into everyday life.
  • Conversion: Community, book club membership, and recurring activities that build loyalty and drive regular purchases.

Core Strategies

  • Enhanced visibility in the Upper Funnel, addressing a previous lack of presence in informative search queries.
  • Improved CTR and internal linking to leverage existing strong rankings in the Lower Funnel.
  • A long-term community focus where engagement and relationships carry as much weight as sales.
Results

The initiative positioned Akademibokhandeln as a relevant source of knowledge for parents, not just a book retailer.

  • Increased organic visibility within children's book-related and informative search queries.
  • Stronger connection between content, community, and actual sales of children's books.
  • Improved conditions for recurring purchases through the book club and loyalty-driven activities.
  • In the long term, this enables a higher share of children's book sales compared to previous years, with goals set for the end of 2026.
Reflection

This project demonstrates how SEO, content, and community can collectively transform a brand's role—from a store to a support system for parenting.

The greatest long-term impact lies not just in rankings or traffic, but in creating sustainable reading joy and lasting relationships. When value for the family increases, sales become a natural result rather than the primary goal.

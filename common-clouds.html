Background

During the year at Berghs, we worked hands-on with Common Clouds to strengthen their organic visibility, search positioning, geo/AI discoverability, and on-site conversion.

Initial analysis showed that most traffic came from branded or informational searches, while visibility in transactional intent and lower-funnel queries remained limited. User-behavior data also revealed friction in the purchase journey, indicating gaps in structure and guidance.

Approach

SEO & Content

  • Strengthened informational content to improve topical authority.
  • Refined metadata and internal linking, connecting blog guides with product pages to unify education and conversion.

GEO & AI Visibility

  • Implemented structured data (FAQ, Article, Product) to help Google and AI systems interpret relevance.
  • Expanded expert-driven content to strengthen E-E-A-T signals.

CRO & UX

  • Reduced friction through mobile-first structure, clearer navigation, and stronger social proof.
  • Introduced micro-conversions like wishlists to capture intent from younger audiences.
Results

The project created a clearer full-funnel ecosystem where informational content builds discovery and trust, while product pages capture demand.

Together, these changes establish stronger long-term conditions for organic growth, AI visibility, and revenue performance rather than short-term traffic gains alone.

Reflection

This work demonstrates how SEO, GEO, and CRO must operate as one connected system. Visibility without usability limits revenue, while conversion optimisation without discoverability limits growth.

By aligning search intent and user experience, Common Clouds moves toward a scalable model where authority and sales reinforce each other.
